About Me
<\header>

My name is Matthew Martin, and I am a third-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Government at the University of Texas at Austin. My subfields of study are public law and comparative politics. Broadly speaking, my research interests include public participation in constitutional design, authoritarian constitutions, judicial politics, and immigration law and politics. I am currently in the early stages of my dissertation, which examines the use of public consultation in constitution-making by authoritarian regimes. Dr. Zachary Elkins is the chair of my dissertation committee.

Prior to graduate school, I received my B.A. in Political Science and Legal Studies along with a certificate in International Relations from the University of Massachusetts at Amherst. Upon graduation, I worked as a legal assistant in immigration law, assisting clients in a wide variety of family- and huminatarian-based cases.
